Grilled Fish Tacos
src: ATK Light and Healthier 2010

Halibut or swordfish can be substituted for the mahi-mahi.

1/2 small head green cabbage (about 8 ounces), cored and shredded (about 4 cups)
5 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
3 scallions, sliced thin
2 tablespoons cider vinegar
1 tablespoon canola oil
Salt 
3/4  cup light mayonnaise
1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
2 teaspoons minced canned chipotle chilies in adobo sauce (I use 1/4 chipotle powder)
1 garlic glove, minced.
pepper 
2 teaspoons chili powder
1/2 teaspoon coriander
1/4 teaspoon cumin
4 (6-ounce) skinless mahi-mahi fillets, about 1 inch thick.
12 (6 inch) corn tortillas, warmed

Toss the cabbage, 1/4 cup of the cilantro, scallions, vinegar, 1 teaspoon of the oil and 1/4 teaspoon salt together in a medium bowl, and set aside for serving. In a separate bowl combine the mayonnaise, reamining1 tabled spoon cilantro, lime juice, chipotles, and garlic. Season with salt and peer to taste.

Combine the chili powder, coriander, cumin 1/8 teaspoon salt and 1/8 teaspoon pepper in a small bowl. Pat the mahi-mahi fillets dry with paper towels, brush with the remaining 2 teaspoons oil, then rube evenly with the spice mixture. Place the mahi-mahi on a wire rack set over a rimmed baking sheet and refrigerate until the grill is read,.

Prepare grill. Clean and oil the cooking grate. Place the mahi-mahi on the grill, perpendicular to the bars of the cooking grate. Cook until the fillets are opaque and flake apart when gently prodded with a paring knife 10 to 14 minutes, gently flipping the fillets halfway through with two spatulas, Transfer the fillets to a carving board, tent loosely with foil and let rest for 5 minutes.

Cut each fillet into 4 equal pieces. Spoon 1 tablespoon of the mayonnaise mixture on each warm tortilla, top with the cabbage salad and a piece of the mahi-mahi, and server
