Slow Cooker Chili

2 tablespoons vegetable oil
3 medium onions
6 medium garlic cloves, minced or pressed though a garlic press (about 2 tablespoons)
1/4 cup chili powder
1/4 cup tomato paste
1 tablespoon ground cumin
1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
Salt
1 (28-ounce) can diced tomatoes
2 pounds (85 percent lean) ground beef
2 (15.5 ounce) cans dark red kidney beans, rinsed and drained
1 (28-ounce) can tomato puree
2-4 tables spoons minced chipotle chile in adobo sauce
3 tablespoons soy sauce
1 tablespoon minced fresh oregano leaves, or 1 teaspoon dried
1 tablespoon brown sugar
Ground black pepper
Lime wedges for serving


Heat the oil in a 12 inch non-stick skillet over medium heat until shimmering but not smoking. add the onions , garlic chili, powder, tomato paste, cumin, pepper flakes and 1/4 teaspoon salt and cook until the onions are softened and lightly browned, 10 to 15 minutes. Stir in the died tomatoes with their juices, scraping up any browned bits,

Transfer the onion and tomato mixture to the slow cooker insert and stri in the beef, beans, tomato puree, chipoltes, soy sauce, oregano, and sugar until evenly combined. Cover and cook on low until the meat is tender an the chili flavorful, 8 to 9 hours (alternatively, cover and cook on high for 5 to 6 hours.)

Gently tilt the slow cooker insert and degrease as much fat as possible off the surface of the chili using a large flat spoon. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve with the lime wedges
