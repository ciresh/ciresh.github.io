Cooks Illustrated Millionaires Shortbread

Crust
2 1/2 Cups (12 1/2 ounces) all purpose flour
1/2 cup (3 1/2 ounces) granulated sugar
3/4 teaspoon salt
16 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Filling
1 (14 ounces) can sweetened condensed milk
1 cup packed (7 ounces) brow sugar
1/2 cup heavy cream
1/2 cup corn syrup
8 tablespoons unsalted butter
1/2 teaspoon salt

Chocolate
8 ounces bittersweet chocolate (6 ounces chopped fine, 2 ounces grated)

1. Preheat over to 350. Make foil sling for 9x13 metal pan. Combine flour sugar, and salt in medium bowl. Add melted butter and stir with rubber spatula until flour is evenly moistened. Crumble dough evenly over bottom of prepared pan. Using your fingerp tips and palm of your hand, press and smooth dough into even thickness. Using fork, pierces  dough at 1 inch intervals. Bake until light golden brown and firm to touch, 25 to 30 minutes. Transfer pan to wire rack. Using sturdy metal spatula, press on entire surface of warm crust to compress (this will make finished bars easier to cut). Let crust cool until it is just warm, at least 20 minutes.

For the Filling: stir all ingredients together in a large, heavy bottom saucepan, Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until mixture registers between 236 and 239 degrees (temperature will fluctuate), 15 to 20 minutes. Pour over crust an spread to even thickness (mixture will be very hot). Let cool completely, about 1 1/2 hours.

For the Chocolate: Microwave chopped chocolate in bowl at 50 percent power, stirring every 15 seconds, until melted but no much warmer than body temperature (check buy holding palm of your hand), 1 to 2 minutes. Add grated chocolate and stir until smooth, returning to microwave for no more the 5 seconds at a time to finish meting if necessary. Spread chocolate evenly over surface of filling. Refrigerate shortbread until chocolate is just set about 10 minutes.

Using foil overhang, lift shourtbreab out of pan transfer to cutting board; discard foil. Using serrated knife and gentle sawing motion, cut shortbread in half crosswise to create two 6 1/2 by 8 inches rectangles. Cut each rectangle in half to make four 3 1/2 by 9 inch strips. Cut each strip crosswise into 10 equal pieces. (Shortbread and stored at room temperature, between layers of parchment, for up to 1 week).
